# Building Board Decks

A comprehensive guide to creating effective board presentations for startup leadership.

## Board Deck Structure

### Executive Summary

A brief overview covering:

- Company performance highlights
- Key decisions requiring board input
- Requests for board assistance

### Financial Performance

- Revenue figures
- Expense tracking
- Profit analysis

### Strategic Goals

- Mission statement
- Vision statement
- Core values

### Company Operations

- Products offered
- Services provided
- Customer base overview

### Risks and Opportunities

- Competitive landscape analysis
- Regulatory environment considerations
- Technological advancement impacts

### Call to Action

Clear statement of what board directors must do next.

## Visuals

- Charts and graphs
- Data tables

## Proofreading

- Verify accuracy
- Ensure clarity and conciseness of visual elements

## Additional Tips

Use clear and concise language. Keep slides visually appealing and easy to understand. Maintain logical organization and flow throughout the presentation.

Be specific when requesting board assistance. Avoid asking the board to interpret quarterly performance, provide data analysis support, or suggest metrics to track.

Use departmental templates to determine which slides are essential versus appendix material.

## Board Meeting Preparation

- Designate a board deck keeper
- Begin deck development well in advance
- Prepare a data summary document
- Focus narrative on key decisions needed
- Conduct team practice presentations
- Distribute deck before the meeting
