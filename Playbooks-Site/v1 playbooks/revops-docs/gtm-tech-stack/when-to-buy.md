# When To Buy New Systems

This document provides guidance for B2B technology companies at different growth stages regarding investment timing for RevOps tools.

## Early Stage (Seed to Series A)

The foundational recommendations include:

1. **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)** - Tools like Salesforce or HubSpot to centralize customer data and enable data-driven decision-making

2. **Marketing Automation** - Platforms such as HubSpot, Marketo, or Pardot for managing outreach, lead nurturing, and campaigns

3. **Contact Data Enrichment** - Services like ZoomInfo, Seamless, or Apollo for obtaining accurate contact information

4. **Sales Engagement Platform** - Solutions including Outreach, Salesloft, or Groove to automate tasks and improve productivity

## Mid-Stage (Series B)

Expansion recommendations include:

1. **Customer Success Platform** - ChurnZero, Gainsight, or Planhat to empower CSMs and enhance retention

2. **Configure Price Quote (CPQ)** - Solutions like DealHub, Salesforce CPQ, or Aptis for streamlining configuration and quoting

3. **Coaching Platform** - Tools such as Gong or Chorus for recording and analyzing sales calls

## Late-Stage (Series C and Beyond)

Advanced investments include:

1. **Revenue Intelligence** - Qlik, BoostUp, or Clari for data analysis and trend identification
2. **Channel Management** - PartnerStack or Allbound for partner relationships
3. **Content Management** - Seismic or Highspot for content distribution
4. **Training and Enablement** - MindTickle or BrainShark for employee training
5. **Commission Management** - Spiff or CaptivateIQ for automating calculations

## Tool Usage by Department

| Department | Tools |
|---|---|
| Sales | CRM, Sales Engagement Platform, Coaching Platform, CPQ |
| Marketing | CRM, Marketing Automation, Contact Data Enrichment |
| Customer Success | CRM, Customer Success Platform |
| Finance | CRM, Commission Management |
| Operations | CRM, Channel Management |
| Content | Content Management |
| Training | Training and Enablement |

:::note
The CRM is the foundation for all these tools and should receive continuous investment to ensure optimal functionality.
:::
